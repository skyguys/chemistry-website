Soap VS Milk & Water

We ran multiple experiments in class concerning the the effects of soap on milk and water. We got to see the different effects that soap had on surface tension in both of these two scenarios. In these experiments, we saw soap make beautiful food coloring art in a puddle of milk and on the other hand completely destroy anything trying to rest on top of water.



For our experiment, we put milk onto a styrofoam plate and put food coloring into four different ends of the plate, having blue, red, green, and yellow. We then returned the food coloring to Pb. We received liquid soap, and upon doing so, we dropped the food coloring into the middle of the plate. We observed the liquids moving around, mixing the different colors into a swirl of beautiful colors. It was quite a pretty reaction. How does this happen? How does adding liquid soap to milk cause the liquids to blend?
