Soap VS Milk & Water

We ran multiple experiments in class concerning the the effects of soap on milk and water. We got to see the different effects that soap had on surface tension in both of these two scenarios. In these experiments, we saw soap make beautiful food coloring art in a puddle of milk and on the other hand completely destroy anything trying to rest on top of water.



For our experiment, we put milk onto a styrofoam plate and put food coloring into four different ends of the plate, having blue, red, green, and yellow. We then returned the food coloring to Pb. We received liquid soap, and upon doing so, we dropped the food coloring into the middle of the plate. We observed the liquids moving around, mixing the different colors into a swirl of beautiful colors. It was quite a pretty reaction. We also observed the difference between whole in skim milk, each showing a drastically different blend of colors. How does this happen? How does adding liquid soap to milk cause the liquids to blend?



How does soap cause such a pretty reaction?? It is the structure of the soap that allows it to blend all of the food coloring together. The molecules of soap have a hydrophobic tail and hydrophilic head. This structure is what allows soap to clean. It is also what allows this amazing reaction to occur. After forming micelles (spheres of hydrophobic tails and hydrophilic tails with the heads on the outside and the tails on the inside), the hydrophobic tails attract the non polar fat molecules and break them down. The micelles then hit the fat molecules, initiating the reaction.



After the reaction begins, soap molecules "chase" the fat molecules in the food colouring all over the plate of milk, causing the colors to blend and swirl. This typically isn't visible to the naked eye, but with the help of food colouring, you can clearly observe what is happening (even if you don't understand). As the soap finishes blending with the milk, this reaction slows down and eventually ceases. The more fat there is in the milk, the better the colours turn out to be and the longer the reaction lasts because there is more fat for the soap molecules to "chase" the fat. Therefore, this reaction would be much better in whole milk rather than skim milk.
